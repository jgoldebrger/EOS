# Headlines & Cascades

Headlines share good news. Cascades communicate important messages from leadership to other teams.

## Headlines

**Path:** `/org/[slug]/teams/[teamSlug]/headlines`

### Types

| Type | Use for |
|------|---------|
| **Customer** | Wins with customers (new deals, praise, retention) |
| **Employee** | Wins with employees (hires, promotions, culture moments) |

### Create a headline

1. Open team **Headlines** (or add during L10 Headlines section).
2. Enter title and details.
3. Select type (customer or employee).

### During L10

The Headlines section runs quick — share 1–2 wins per person without discussion.

---

## Cascading messages

Cascades spread leadership communication to other teams.

### From headlines

1. Create or open a headline.
2. Enable **cascading**.
3. Select target teams.
4. Send — recipients get **inbox items** to read and acknowledge.

### From L10 Conclude

At the end of a meeting, the **cascading messages checklist** lets facilitators send messages to sibling teams.

### Acknowledging cascades

1. Open **Inbox**.
2. Find the cascade item.
3. Read and **acknowledge**.

Completion is tracked in **Reports** (cascade drill-down).

### Email

Cascade recipients may also receive email if they have assignment notifications enabled in their profile.

---

## Best practices

- Keep headlines brief — wins, not war stories.
- Use cascades sparingly for messages that truly need cross-team alignment.
- Acknowledge cascades promptly so leadership sees completion in Reports.
